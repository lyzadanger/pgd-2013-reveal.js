A bit of self-defense

PhoneGap Self-defense for those of us who think like the web
I came out the other side with a better understanding, and some psychic bruises. And maybe I can help you have a smoother ride.

Where we go wrong

If you are a web developer about to make your first forays into PhoneGap, here are some areas you may want to wear some extra padding.

Core Web Concepts

Adherence to certain core web concepts can get us in trouble. Some things we have long held to be Absolutely The Way are different in the app world, subtlely or extremely.

We have certain patterns of thinking about the way the web works as a whole, about the way that Web standards work together. These patterns are often skewed in the native application context.

Nuance and Best Practices

Like it or not, we're on device in a native context.

As web developers, tend to fight platform specificity. It's in our nature. But we are in a native context. And because of that, we're beholden to certain patterns that will be new to us.

Process, Components and Tools

Suddenly, your workflow matters. A trusty text editor isn't enough anymore, there's tons of tedious detail you need to deal with

I'm somewhat irrationally proud of my "have text editor, will travel", environment-free way of developing and that no longer really cuts it. Suddenly I have to buckle down and deal with config-level tedium that does not generally appeal to me as a web dev. But giving it short shrift can be fatal.

Complexity

And these problems compound as your app grows.

It's self-evident that these concerns would get more complex as your app grows. So I urge you to get out in front of things beforehand.

You'll be Wrangling Data

  • Caching
  • Synching
  • Offline mode

Wrangling Data: Caching

  • Default to keeping stuff locally
  • I don't mind using localStorage...what?
  • Don't depend on localStorage
  • Lots of stuff? You probably want to use IndexedDB or something cool like that.

Wrangling Data: Synching

Your models are dirty, dirty, dirty

  • Synching local dirty data and doing a good job of it is a non-trivial problem
  • I'm not smart enough to tackle it myself, so I use things like backbone.dualStorage
  • Make it behave RESTfully just like remote sync to keep the chaos minimized
  • Nervous? You might consider readonly for local

Wrangling Data: Offline Mode

Finally a bit of good news: build your data wrangling carefully, and offline mode can follow naturally.

WRT PhoneGap proper, If you want a bit more control:

Application Context: A potpourri of platform-specific details you now need to care about

We're no longer safe inside generic, universal browser chrome. We have to care about device and platform specifics to get our jobs done. We have to care about the interface.

Potpourri: Interface

Here is just one random example of things you now need to care about:

  • Keyboards
    • Soft or hard?
    • How to trigger?
    • How to get the right one?
    • How to make it go away?
    • How big is it?
    • How has its appearance affected the viewport?
    • How to add a magic-y submit button-y thing

MOAR Interface Potpourri

It is up to you, intrepid developer, to make sure that things behave acceptably.

e.g.

  • fastclick.js for eliminating 300ms tap delays
  • statusTap plugin on iOS
  • Various plugins for making third-party social authentication tolerable
It's often up to you to make the interface work better.
